Analysis

My question, how the total arrests have changed over time, can be answered through the graphs. The line graph shows that the total arrests over time has gone down throughout the years. The bar graph also shows this by showing the bars going down, with bigger ones at the start and smaller ones at the end. Something that I noticed is that around 1999 and 2000, the arrests went down significantly. A limitation on this data is that it doesn't explain why the arrests went down, but just that they did. There could have been law or policy changed that made this but from this data we do not know. Also the data is for the entire US, which makes it hard to determine crime rates/arrests in different areas. For further investigations, I would look at smaller population areas, like the city of Eugene, or a state, and look at per 100,000 people.
